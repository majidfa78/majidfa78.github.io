About Me

Hi! I'm Majid, a digital electronics researcher and embedded systems developer who loves building things at the intersection of hardware and AI. I am fascinated by how systems think, sense, and learn, and my journey has been dedicated to turning abstract concepts into functional devices.

My expertise ranges from low-level MCU programming and digital circuit design to neuromorphic models and deep learning. I thrive on projects that combine creativity with engineering precision—whether designing BLE/UWB localization systems, implementing hardware-efficient neural networks, or teaching deep learning to graduate students.

Currently, I am focused on memory design, in-memory computing, and intelligent hardware architectures. When not debugging firmware or designing architectures, I'm exploring new ML ideas or developing side projects.
