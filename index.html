About

Hi! I'm Majid — a digital electronics researcher, embedded systems developer, and AI enthusiast who enjoys building things that sit at the intersection of hardware and intelligent software. I've always been fascinated by how systems think, sense, and learn, and a big part of my journey has been turning those ideas into real, functional devices.

Over the years, I've worked on everything from low-level MCU programming and digital circuit design to neuromorphic models and deep learning applications. I love projects that combine creativity with engineering — whether it's designing a precise BLE and UWB indoor-Localization system, implementing a hardware-efficient neural network, or teaching deep learning to graduate students.

I'm currently focused on memory design, in-memory computing, and intelligent hardware architectures. My broader interests include IoT systems, indoor localization systems, long-range wireless communication, and practical AI for real-world devices.

Professional Bio

Majid Farajian, born in 1999 in Ahvaz, Iran, is a digital electronic engineer with strong experience in embedded systems, AI-focused hardware design, and indoor localization technologies. He earned his B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering-Electronics from Shahid Chamran University of Ahvaz and his M.Sc. in Digital Electronic Engineering from Shahid Beheshti University.

Majid has over a decade of hands-on R&D experience in electronic circuit design, firmware development, and IoT systems. He previously worked as a Senior Embedded Developer at CoolTag Company and serves as a Graduate Instructional Assistant for advanced AI courses at KNTU.
